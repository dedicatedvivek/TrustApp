SAR STEREO MATCHING



Martin Davison is pleased to present some results that indicate the enormous potential of SYNTHETIC APERTURE RADAR (SAR) stereo matching for the creation of DIGITAL ELEVATION MODELS (DEM). These results have been generated using the ALTUS system, written and developed by Martin Davison.

By measuring the disparity of features between a pair of SAR images of the same region, but observed at different viewing angles, ALTUS is able to create an accurate and detailed elevation model of the imaged terrain. ALTUS can achieve an absolute accuracy in position of 50 metres and 20 metres in height, equivalent to the accuracy of the satellite orbit. However, the inclusion of local control points within the imagery can considerably improve this. Relative heights across different regions within the terrain model can be measured to within 5 metres.

This powerful system benefits from the all weather capabilities of SAR, which can penetrate cloud and tropical haze. For this reason, the system is particularly suited to DEM generation over remote, tropical forest regions, where no other imagery or technique can be applied due to the high humidity and consequent persistent cloud cover.
